# Jwu Studio Wordmark Directions

Three De Stijl / Bauhaus-inspired directions for the `Jwu Studio` mark, tuned for the current portfolio system.

## 1. Grid Stamp

- Visual logic: a strict rectangular lockup where `Jwu` and `Studio` sit on a shared grid with one hard divider.
- Typography / form: condensed geometric sans, wide tracking, and a small inset rule or square marker to make the mark feel specimen-like.
- Color usage: black text on the off-white field, with one primary-color accent only in the divider or marker.
- Masthead / hero: works best as a compact top-left brand stamp, or as a hero label that can be nudged off-grid without losing structure.

## 2. Split Axis

- Visual logic: the name is cut across a central vertical or horizontal axis, so the mark feels like two balanced planes rather than one continuous line.
- Typography / form: strong sans-serif with alternating weight, a deliberate line break, and a subtle shift in baseline between `Jwu` and `Studio`.
- Color usage: primary red for one half, blue or yellow for the other, with black support lines keeping the composition grounded.
- Masthead / hero: ideal for a movable masthead treatment where the wordmark can be dragged slightly out of alignment and still feel composed.

## 3. Specimen Block

- Visual logic: a type-specimen panel that frames `Jwu Studio` inside a larger geometric block, like a modernist poster fragment.
- Typography / form: oversized wordmark, tight vertical stacking, and a secondary micro-label or index number to make it feel curated.
- Color usage: one dominant primary field behind the wordmark, with black type and white negative space to preserve clarity.
- Masthead / hero: strongest for a hero treatment or landing-state emblem, where the wordmark becomes a central object rather than a small brand label.

## Recommendation

- Use `Grid Stamp` for the masthead.
- Use `Split Axis` for the draggable / interactive hero variation.
- Use `Specimen Block` for a larger landing-state composition or promotional lockup.
